Insurance brokers spend hours reading contracts, filling carrier portals, and comparing quotes in spreadsheets. What if all of that could happen in minutes? This is Lumify.

This is an insurance broker's ops dashboard. The entire workflow is bilingual, Spanish and English, with a single toggle. A new request just came in from Constructora del Pacifico, a 500 million peso highway project that needs both insurance and surety bonds.

The broker starts in Gmail. The client has sent the master services contract, 45 pages of dense legal Spanish with insurance and bond requirements buried throughout.

Lumify automatically picks up the contract from Gmail and brings it into the platform. No downloading, no printing, no manual steps.

The system pulls up everything it knows about this client: company profile, financial data, risk metrics, existing policies. The broker can also upload additional documents right here, multiple files at once, anything the underwriters will need later.

Lumify reads both documents, the master services contract and the bond requirements addendum, extracting every insurance and surety requirement.

Six insurance requirements extracted, each mapped to a specific contract clause. Three surety bonds identified from the bond addendum.

Here's where it gets interesting. This requirement, Contractors All Risk, was buried in clause fourteen point three point two, in the Special Provisions section, not in the main Insurance section. Lumify flags it: detected in deep clause, manual review could miss this. On a 500 million peso highway project, missing this coverage would be catastrophic.

The system compares contract requirements against the client's existing coverage. Green means covered, red means a gap. Construction Equipment has a 112 million peso shortfall. All three surety bonds are entirely new. These gaps define exactly what needs to go to market.

Based on the risk profile, the system selects six carriers: four for insurance, two surety specialists. Each carrier shows the submission method, portal or email, and why. Mapfre goes through their agent portal. Chubb exceeds portal limits, so it routes to email. Six carriers, two channels, all handled from one screen.

For portal carriers, the system fills every form field automatically: project name, insured value, policy period, all extracted from the contract. Supporting documents attach one by one. The broker reviews, confirms, and it's submitted.

For email carriers, the system drafts personalized submission emails, each one tailored to the specific carrier with the right documents attached. The broker can edit any email directly on the platform, add or remove attachments, and adjust the language before sending. Once it looks right, click Send, and the email goes out right from the platform. Four carriers, four clicks, done.

Back in Gmail, four submission emails sent. Combined with the two portal submissions, that's six carriers submitted to in under ten minutes. The entire admin burden of going to market, handled.

As quotes come back from carriers, the system tracks every response: who replied, when, and who still needs follow up.

Three of four insurance quotes have arrived. The broker doesn't have to wait for the rest. They can click Compare at any point to generate a side by side comparison with whatever quotes are in so far.

Now all six quotes are in, four insurance, two surety, and Lumify automatically generates the full comparison.

The system compares every quote against every contract requirement. Not just price, but coverage. Every deductible, every limit, every exclusion.

And here's the critical catch. Zurich's quote has an express exclusion for vibration damage. This is a 167 kilometer highway project with heavy earthmoving, blasting, and pile driving. The contract requires this coverage in clause nine point one. A broker comparing four PDF quotes side by side could easily miss this buried on page twelve of Zurich's terms. The system catches it instantly.

Mapfre comes in at the lowest premium, 847,000 pesos, with full coverage and no critical exclusions.

For surety bonds, Aserta meets 100 percent of required amounts with a pre-approved credit line. Dorama falls short on the quality guarantee bond, 45 million versus 50 million required. That five million peso gap would be a contractual violation.

The quote comparison goes to the client, a professional email from the brokerage with the full analysis attached. The client sees the broker's brand, not the AI. Lumify is invisible infrastructure.

From receiving the contract to uncovering what's required and delivering the quote comparison, what used to take hours, done in minutes. Six carriers, two channels, three surety bonds, one critical exclusion caught. And when this policy renews next year, all the context is already here. That's Lumify for insurance brokers.
